Look Back (ルックバック) by Tatsuki Fujimoto

Look Back (Japanese: ルックバック) is a Japanese one-shot web manga written and illustrated by Tatsuki Fujimoto. It was published on Shueisha's Shōnen Jump+ in July 2021. It tells the story of Ayumu Fujino, a young manga artist who, driven by rivalry and friendship with a reclusive classmate, strives to improve her craft and finds purpose in creating art. An anime film adaptation produced by Studio Durian premiered in June 2024.

Kyomoto is the deuteragonist of the manga one-shot series Look Back. She is an introverted girl who has a great passion into drawing and met Ayumu Fujino.

Plot (Spoiler Warning)

Look Back Plot Summary

Ayumu Fujino, an elementary school manga prodigy, publishes her work in the school paper and gains widespread acclaim. Her dominance is challenged by Kyomoto, a reclusive yet highly talented illustrator whose superior work pushes Fujino into a competitive obsession. This fixation on surpassing Kyomoto alienates Fujino from her loved ones and ultimately leads her to quit drawing when she falls short. Tasked with delivering Kyomoto's diploma after middle school graduation, Fujino meets Kyomoto, who reveals herself as a devoted fan of Fujino's work. Inspired by Kyomoto's admiration, Fujino rekindles her passion for manga, and the two become collaborators.

The duo produces acclaimed one-shots, but as teenagers, their paths diverge. Fujino secures a manga serialization for Shark Kick, while Kyomoto chooses to pursue formal art education. Fujino thrives, with her manga even receiving an anime adaptation. However, tragedy strikes when Kyomoto becomes a victim in a mass murder at her art college. Wracked with guilt, Fujino blames herself for encouraging Kyomoto to pursue art, believing this indirectly led to her death.

Haunted by regret, Fujino visits Kyomoto's home and tears up a yonkoma comic she had once drawn mocking her. A fragment of the comic seemingly time-travels to their middle school days, altering events. Kyomoto, now too startled by the comic to leave her room, still finds motivation to pursue art on her own. In the altered timeline, Fujino saves Kyomoto during the attack and reconnects with her. Kyomoto expresses her admiration for Fujino, and the two discuss creating manga together before Fujino is taken away by ambulance.

In the original timeline, Fujino discovers a yonkoma created by Kyomoto depicting her rescue and finds it alongside Kyomoto's preserved copies of Shark Kick. Realizing Kyomoto never stopped admiring her work, Fujino wrestles with despair over her career. However, memories of the happiness her manga brought to Kyomoto reignite her resolve. Inspired by Kyomoto's enduring faith in her, Fujino returns to drawing, determined to honor their shared love for manga.

Inspiration Behind Look Back and the Art of Tatsuki Fujimoto

Tatsuki Fujimoto's Look Back is deeply inspired by his own struggles as an artist and his reflections following the 2011 Tohoku Earthquake. During this time, Fujimoto grappled with feelings of powerlessness, questioning the purpose of art in the face of tragedy. These emotions fueled the story's central themes of resilience and finding meaning in creativity. Like Fujino in the story, Fujimoto realized that while art may not prevent tragedy, it can bring joy and connection to others, offering hope in difficult times. At its heart, Look Back is a celebration of art's power to communicate and inspire. Fujimoto's reflections on his own artistic journey, from despair to rediscovering the joy of creation, are mirrored in the story's narrative. The manga encourages readers to embrace the value of their work, showing that even in a world filled with tragedy, art has the power to uplift and connect people on a profound level.

About the author

Tatsuki Fujimoto (Japanese: 藤本 タツキ, Hepburn: Fujimoto Tatsuki, born October 10, 1992, or 1993[a]) is a Japanese manga artist, known for his works Fire Punch and Chainsaw Man.

Tatsuki Fujimoto was born in Nikaho, Akita Prefecture, in 1992 or 1993. Without access to preparatory schools, he practiced oil painting in classes attended by his grandparents. He developed his artistic talents early and pursued formal education in art, graduating in Western painting from Tohoku University of Art and Design in 2014. His upbringing in a rural area influenced his creative journey, leading him to explore unconventional paths in his artistic development.

Fujimoto began his manga career with one-shot works such as A Couple Clucking Chickens Were Still Kickin' in the Schoolyard (2011) and Kami Hikōki (2013), earning awards and recognition early on. He serialized his first major work, Fire Punch (2016-2018), on Shōnen Jump+ before achieving global acclaim with Chainsaw Man (2018-2020), which won several prestigious awards, including multiple Harvey Awards. Fujimoto continued to create acclaimed one-shots like Look Back (2021) and Goodbye, Eri (2022), further solidifying his reputation as an innovative storyteller.

Fujimoto's storytelling is deeply influenced by cinema, particularly Korean films like The Chaser and Japanese works such as Sadako vs. Kayako. He admired the unpredictable narratives of directors like Takeshi Kitano and was inspired by the tension and pacing of films like The Raid. His manga influences include Hiroaki Samura, Hideki Arai, and Tsutomu Nihei, whose distinctive styles shaped his approach to blending emotional depth with subversive, cinematic twists.
